# Ocean Expeditions Atlas

![preview](../../assets/public-previews/ocean-expeditions-atlas.svg)

This atlas maps expedition ecosystems that shape how humanity explores the ocean, shares discoveries, and connects seafloor science with public imagination.

## Why This Matters

Ocean expeditions are not only voyages. They are operating systems for discovery, mapping, sampling, imaging, education, live telepresence, and public trust. They also help build the bridge from the ocean of Earth to the ocean worlds of space.

## Historical Public Exploration Line

- [The Cousteau Society / Calypso](https://www.cousteau.org/legacy/vessels/calypso/) — the iconic public expedition line that turned ocean exploration into a global cultural language.

## Live Ocean Exploration Ecosystems

- [NOAA Ocean Exploration](https://oceanexplorer.noaa.gov/) — a core public exploration program focused on the unknown ocean.
- [NOAA Ship Okeanos Explorer](https://oceanexplorer.noaa.gov/okeanos/about/) — the U.S. federal ship dedicated to ocean exploration and telepresence-enabled discovery.
- [Schmidt Ocean Institute](https://schmidtocean.org/) — global deep-sea expeditions, open science support, and advanced mapping and ROV operations.
- [Schmidt Ocean Institute Expeditions](https://schmidtocean.org/cruises/schmidt-ocean-institute-2025-expeditions/) — a clear expedition entry point for current field seasons.
- [OceanX](https://oceanx.org/) — expedition science combined with media, storytelling, and public engagement.
- [Ocean Exploration Trust / Nautilus Live](https://nautiluslive.org/) — a live expedition model linking telepresence, education, robotics, archaeology, geology, and biology.
- [E/V Nautilus](https://nautiluslive.org/tech/ev-nautilus) — a flagship vessel in the public telepresence exploration ecosystem.
- [MBARI Expeditions](https://www.mbari.org/expeditions/) — a strong model at the intersection of marine science, engineering, imaging, and long-horizon fieldwork.
- [Ocean Census Expeditions](https://oceancensus.org/expeditions/) — a global expedition line oriented toward species discovery and ocean biodiversity.

## Signals Ocean Fund Should Study

- how expeditions explain uncertainty instead of hiding it;
- how mapping, ROV video, and sample work become public knowledge;
- how live participation and education are integrated into the mission;
- how vessels, labs, media, and philanthropy work together;
- how expedition culture can inform ocean-worlds and planetary-ocean exploration.

## Ocean Fund Direction

Ocean Fund should treat expeditions as a major public knowledge layer: not just a list of voyages, but a map of institutions, technologies, ships, crews, media systems, discovery pipelines, and public-learning architectures.
