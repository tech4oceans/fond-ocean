# Ocean Worlds Community Routes

![preview](../assets/public-previews/ocean-worlds-community-routes.svg)

This page maps the community routes Ocean Fund should follow across ocean worlds, astrobiology, planetary water, and the wider space-ocean imagination.

Verified against official public sources on 13 May 2026.

## Why This Layer Exists

Ocean Fund does not stop at Earth-ocean communities. Its long arc includes the ocean worlds of the Solar System and the public, scientific, and mission communities built around them.

## Core Community Routes

- [NASA Ocean Worlds](https://science.nasa.gov/solar-system/ocean-worlds): central framing layer for planetary oceans.
- [Network for Ocean Worlds (NOW)](https://science.nasa.gov/astrobiology/researchers/rcns/now/): research coordination around analog environments and comparative ocean-world science.
- [NASA Astrobiology](https://astrobiology.nasa.gov/about/): habitability, life, planetary environments, and interdisciplinary entry routes.
- [Europa Clipper](https://europa.nasa.gov/mission/about/): mission-facing community around Europa and habitability.
- [Dragonfly](https://science.nasa.gov/mission/dragonfly/science/): Titan route linking chemistry, atmosphere, and prebiotic questions.
- [OPAG](https://www.lpi.usra.edu/opag/): outer planets and ocean worlds advisory community.
- [VEXAG](https://www.lpi.usra.edu/vexag/): Venus exploration forum.
- [MExAG](https://www.lpi.usra.edu/mexag/): Mercury exploration community.
- [LEAG](https://www.lpi.usra.edu/leag/): lunar exploration advisory group.

## What Ocean Fund Should Do Here

- follow public talks, resources, and mailing-list routes;
- learn how planetary communities connect science, mission storytelling, and public imagination;
- keep building the bridge between Earth-ocean literacy and the wider family of ocean worlds.

## Working Rule

Ocean worlds are not a side topic. They are part of the long public language of water, habitability, exploration, and planetary futures.
