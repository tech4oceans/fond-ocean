# Ocean Nonprofit and Giving Models

This page tracks nonprofit ocean organizations whose public donation systems, membership structures, and supporter pathways are useful reference points for Ocean Fund.

Verified against official sites on 12 May 2026.

## Why This Layer Exists

Ocean Fund will eventually need a trustworthy public support system. That means studying how leading nonprofit ocean organizations structure giving, memberships, monthly donors, fundraising campaigns, legacy gifts, and corporate support.

## Reference Organizations

- [Ocean Conservancy](https://oceanconservancy.org/): one-time and monthly gifts, fundraising toolkit, crypto giving, donor circle, and legacy options.
- [Oceana](https://oceana.org/waystogive): one-time giving, monthly community, membership framing, and tribute-style options.
- [Surfrider Foundation](https://www.surfrider.org/give): donation-linked membership, multiple payment rails, crypto, fundraising, and chapter-driven community identity.
- [The Ocean Cleanup](https://theoceancleanup.com/donate/): streamlined one-time/monthly form, multi-currency support, tribute fields, and company-giving pathway.
- [Mission Blue](https://missionblue.org/invest-new/): recurring giving, commemorative gifts, Blue Circle membership, campaigns, and legacy framing.
- [Coral Reef Alliance](https://coral.org/en/donate/other/): direct giving plus DAF, stock, QCD, matching, planned giving, and personal fundraising.
- [Reef Check](https://www.reefcheck.org/donate/): membership, gift membership, monthly giving, adopt-a-reef, sponsorship, IRA rollover, stock, bequests, and vehicle giving.
- [Oceanic Society](https://www.oceanicsociety.org/donate/): monthly membership, animal adoption, travel-linked support, legacy, corporate giving, crypto, and stock.

## Donation Design Patterns Worth Studying

- simple one-time and monthly giving on the main public page;
- supporter identity through membership, circles, or named communities;
- tribute, memorial, or campaign-based giving;
- peer-to-peer fundraising and social fundraising kits;
- advanced giving paths such as DAF, stock, QCD, bequests, or legacy societies;
- mission-linked giving routes such as adoption, expeditions, cleanup campaigns, or sponsor circles.

## What Ocean Fund Should Learn

- how to make giving visible without making the project feel extractive;
- how to connect donations to clear public outcomes;
- how to build recurring support, not only one-off gifts;
- how to combine grassroots participation with larger donor pathways;
- how to keep public trust through clarity, restraint, and mission discipline.

## Working Rule

Ocean Fund should borrow structural lessons, not copy claims, numbers, legal flows, or emotional framing without adaptation.
