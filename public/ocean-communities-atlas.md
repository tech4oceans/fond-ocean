# Ocean Communities Atlas

This page maps the communities Ocean Fund should watch, learn from, and eventually enter across Earth oceans, climate systems, marine data, and ocean worlds in space.

Verified against official sites on 12 May 2026.

## Why This Layer Exists

Ocean Fund needs more than content. It needs a map of living communities: scientific networks, open-data infrastructures, activist networks, planetary forums, and recurring event routes.

## Earth Ocean Communities

- [Ocean Decade](https://oceandecade.org/): global UN coordination for ocean science, action, and public engagement.
- [GenOcean](https://oceandecade.org/genocean/): public mobilization and citizen-facing Ocean Decade campaign.
- [GOOS](https://goosocean.org/what-we-do/): global observing community for sustained ocean monitoring.
- [OBIS](https://portal.obis.org/about/): open marine biodiversity data community with distributed nodes.
- [Copernicus Marine](https://marine.copernicus.eu/about): operational marine data, forecasting, and public services.
- [EMODnet](https://emodnet.ec.europa.eu/en/about-emodnet): interoperable European marine data network.
- [Ocean Conservancy](https://oceanconservancy.org/), [Oceana](https://oceana.org/waystogive), [Surfrider](https://www.surfrider.org/join), [Mission Blue](https://missionblue.org/partners/), [Reef Check](https://www.reefcheck.org/), [Coral Reef Alliance](https://coral.org/en/impact/), and [Oceanic Society](https://www.oceanicsociety.org/donate/) show how public-action communities are built around campaigns, memberships, chapters, expeditions, or partner coalitions.

## Space and Planetary Ocean Communities

- [NASA Ocean Worlds](https://science.nasa.gov/solar-system/ocean-worlds): the core framing layer for planetary oceans and water worlds.
- [NASA Astrobiology](https://astrobiology.nasa.gov/about/): research and education community around life, habitability, and planetary environments.
- [Moon to Mars](https://science.nasa.gov/toolkit/moon-to-mars/): public science and exploration route connecting lunar and martian communities.
- [LEAG](https://www.lpi.usra.edu/leag/LEAG_ToR_022025_Final.pdf): community-based lunar exploration forum.
- [Mars Exploration Program](https://science.nasa.gov/planetary-science/programs/mars-exploration/): Mars science goals, workshops, technology, and future planning.
- [VEXAG](https://www.lpi.usra.edu/vexag/): open Venus exploration forum.
- [MExAG](https://www.lpi.usra.edu/mexag/): Mercury exploration group with open participation and mailing-list entry.
- [OPAG](https://www.lpi.usra.edu/opag/): outer planets and ocean-worlds community forum.
- [Europa Clipper](https://europa.nasa.gov/mission/about/): mission-facing community around one of the most important ocean worlds in the Solar System.

## Practical Entry Paths for Ocean Fund

- track newsletters, mailing lists, public meetings, and open resource pages;
- convert each target community into a partner card, source card, and next-step issue;
- build bridges between Earth-ocean communities and ocean-world communities through public language, data pages, and event packs;
- treat climate, ecology, marine data, and space-ocean communities as one connected field rather than isolated silos.

## Working Rule

Ocean Fund should learn from these communities systematically, but describe relationships carefully: watching, learning from, or mapping a community is not the same as a formal partnership.
