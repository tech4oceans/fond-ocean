# Climate and Ecology Partner Map

![preview](../assets/public-previews/climate-and-ecology-partner-map.svg)

This page maps organizations and alliance structures that sit at the intersection of ocean, climate, biodiversity, wetlands, resilience, and coastal ecosystems.

Verified against official public sources on 13 May 2026.

## Why This Layer Exists

Ocean Fund should not treat ocean work as isolated from climate and ecology. The strongest partner routes often sit exactly at that intersection.

## Core Partner Routes

- [The Nature Conservancy - Oceans and Coasts Network](https://www.nature.org/en-us/what-we-do/our-priorities/protect-water-and-land/land-and-water-stories/ocean-coasts-network/): blue carbon, living shorelines, coral restoration, marine mapping, fisheries, and resilience.
- [Conservation International - Center for Oceans](https://www.conservation.org/about/center-for-oceans/2): ocean protection, marine biodiversity, and policy action.
- [WWF - Ocean and Climate](https://wwf.panda.org/discover/our_focus/oceans_practice/ocean_and_climate/): climate-ocean linkage, resilience, and conservation framing.
- [Wetlands International - Coasts and Deltas](https://www.wetlands.org/our-work/coasts-and-deltas/): coastal wetlands, sea-level rise, adaptation, and delta resilience.
- [Blue Nature Alliance](https://www.bluenaturealliance.org/about/mission-values/): large-scale protection route linking biodiversity, communities, and finance.
- [ORRAA](https://oceanriskalliance.org/project/ocean-resilience-innovation-challenge/): finance and innovation route for coastal and marine resilience.

## What Ocean Fund Should Learn

- climate and ecology partner maps need both conservation actors and resilience-finance actors;
- coastal wetlands, coral systems, fisheries, and blue carbon belong on one strategic map;
- the strongest collaborations often connect science, policy, communities, and finance at once.

## Working Rule

Ocean Fund should build this map as a partnership field, not as a trophy wall.
