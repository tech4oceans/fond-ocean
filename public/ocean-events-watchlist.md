# Ocean Events Watchlist

![preview](../assets/public-previews/ocean-events-watchlist.svg)

This page tracks high-signal ocean events that Ocean Fund should study, monitor, and eventually enter through participation, submissions, side events, public materials, and partnership outreach.

Verified against official public sources on 13 May 2026.

## Why This Layer Exists

Ocean Fund needs a living route into the global ocean calendar: policy forums, science congresses, finance gatherings, and technology exhibitions.

## Current Watchlist

- [UN Ocean Conference 2025](https://sdgs.un.org/conferences/ocean2025/about-unoc-2025): policy anchor. Held in Nice, France, 9-13 June 2025.
- [One Ocean Science Congress 2025](https://one-ocean-science-2025.org/about/information_and_contact.html): science anchor connected to UNOC. Nice, France, 3-6 June 2025.
- [Blue Economy and Finance Forum 2026](https://www.beffmonaco.org/): ocean finance and philanthropy route. Monaco, 28-29 May 2026.
- [Our Ocean Conference 2026](https://www.ouroceanconference.org/conferences/mombasa-2026/): commitments, diplomacy, and delivery route. Mombasa-Kilifi Counties, Kenya, 16-18 June 2026.
- [Ocean Sciences Meeting 2026](https://www.agu.org/Ocean-Sciences-Meeting/): flagship science conference. Glasgow, Scotland, 22-27 February 2026.
- [All-Atlantic Forum 2026](https://allatlanticocean.org/events/2026-all-atlantic-ocean-research-and-innovation-alliance-forum/): alliance-building route across Atlantic science and innovation. Salvador, Bahia, Brazil, 15-16 April 2026.
- [OCEANS 2026 Monterey](https://monterey26.oceansconference.org/): marine technology and engineering route. Monterey Conference Center, 21-24 September 2026.
- [Ocean Business 2027](https://www.oceanbusiness.com/): hands-on ocean technology exhibition route. Southampton, UK, 6-8 April 2027.

## What Ocean Fund Should Do With This

- identify where Ocean Fund should attend, submit, watch, or map only;
- prepare event one-pagers, speaker packs, and partner outreach before deadlines;
- treat each event as an entry route into a specific community, not just as a calendar item.

## Working Rule

An event belongs on this page only if it creates a real route into ocean policy, science, finance, technology, or public culture.
