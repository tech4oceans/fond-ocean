# Ocean Foundations and Donor Architecture

![preview](../assets/public-previews/ocean-foundations-and-donor-architecture.svg)

This page maps the philanthropic and funder-side architecture that Ocean Fund should study when designing its own long-term support model.

Verified against official public sources on 13 May 2026.

## Why This Layer Exists

Ocean Fund already has a public-donation reference page. This layer goes one level higher: foundations, collaboratives, and large-scale funding architectures that shape ocean conservation and ocean-climate strategy.

## Reference Foundations and Funding Architectures

- [Bloomberg Philanthropies - Protecting the Ocean](https://www.bloomberg.org/environment/protecting-the-oceans/): data-driven initiative launched in 2014, working with governments, nonprofits, and research institutions.
- [Packard Foundation - Ocean Initiative](https://www.packard.org/initiative/ocean-initiative/): long-horizon initiative focused on habitats, fisheries, rights, and ocean-climate solutions.
- [Gordon and Betty Moore Foundation - Marine Conservation](https://www.moore.org/programs/environmental-conservation/marine-conservation-initiative): conservation strategy tied to ecosystem resilience, fisheries, and regional focus.
- [Oceans 5](https://www.oceans5.org/about/): funders collaborative making direct grants, matching grants, and strategic guidance for marine reserves and overfishing constraints.
- [Blue Nature Alliance](https://www.bluenaturealliance.org/about/mission-values/): protection-focused alliance led by Conservation International, The Pew Charitable Trusts, the Global Environment Facility, Minderoo Foundation, and the Rob Walton Foundation.
- [Minderoo - OceanOmics](https://cdn.minderoo.org/resources/oceanomics/): example of mission-linked scientific philanthropy through marine genomics and eDNA.

## What Ocean Fund Should Learn

- multi-year strategy matters more than one-off campaigns;
- technical assistance and thought partnership can matter as much as grants;
- collaboratives create leverage that single institutions often cannot;
- donor architecture should connect money, evidence, community legitimacy, and long-term governance.

## Working Rule

Ocean Fund should study structures, time horizons, and mechanisms, not imitate another institution's scale or claims.
